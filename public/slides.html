05 Technical

Pavilion, tents, utilities, and visitor comfort.

The operating plan shows that the experience is not only attractive, but properly supported on the ground.

VM2026 Pavilion

20' x 20' Arabian tent with connected VIP hospitality.

A branded pavilion with information counter, facade treatment, and adjoining modular guest lounge for protocol, sponsors, and media engagement.

Exhibitor Infrastructure

A-frame tents for tourism, craft, and community commerce.

Uniform facades and zone planning keep the proposal from feeling visually messy while giving local sellers enough presence.

Utilities

Generator sets, portable toilets, waste systems, and cleaning workflows.

Power, sanitation, RORO bins, 120L waste bins, and practical furniture selections are built into the operating model from the start.

A stronger proposal language comes from showing infrastructure as designed hospitality, not temporary clutter.
Night lighting and wayfinding keep the festival photogenic after sunset.

06 Program

Sports, crafts, and community participation.

These programs widen the audience beyond passive spectators and help each festival feel active from morning until night.

Fun Run Cuti-Cuti Malaysia MADANI

Beachside fun run with local delicacy checkpoints.

Participants receive VM2026 goodies bags, bib numbers, and medals. Checkpoints feature cekodok, keropok lekor, pisang goreng, and kuih. Traditional costume prizes encouraged. In collaboration with Kementerian Belia dan Sukan.

Craft Culture

Handicraft demonstrations and artisan showcases.

Batik painting, songket weaving, wood carving, and live-making sessions create deeper cultural legitimacy.

Sukan Rakyat

Community sports for all ages and races.

Tarik tali, tarik upih, lumba tempurung, lari dalam guni, bowling kelapa, terompah gergasi. In collaboration with government agencies and local community representatives.
